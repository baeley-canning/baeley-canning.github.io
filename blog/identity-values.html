Identity-Values



I would like to share my personal journey of discovering my values, strengths, and limitations.
Through reflection and self-awareness, I have identified my top 5 signature strengths as fairness, love, judgement, creativity, and love of learning.
Additionally, I have found that I am mildly extroverted, highly open-minded, and very conscientious.
One of the most significant situations where I had to make an ethical decision was when a staff member and I made a joint mistake.
As he was leaving, he asked me to blame him for the mistake, but I disagreed and took responsibility for my actions.
I discussed the situation with our boss and made it clear that it was a joint mistake and that I refused to let my workmate take the fall.
This decision reflects my value of fairness and my refusal to sacrifice the reputation of another person for my own benefit.
I have always believed that my values and identity are self-determined, but I have come to realize that this is not entirely true.
The people around me, including my partner, have undoubtedly influenced my values and identity.
For example, my partner's kindness and caring outlook on life have had a significant impact on me, and I am grateful for the positive influence she has had on me.
In terms of my strengths, I have found that my persistence and directness have supported me in my learning journey.
I push myself to the best of my ability and strive to get straight to the point, which helps me form clear and firm ideas and plans.
However, I have also identified some limitations that could affect my learning journey, such as my tendency to fixate on a task until it is complete, which can make it difficult to multitask.
An example of a situation where I faced resistance or tension while working with others was in a year 8 cooking class where I was baking cupcakes.
One of my team members refused to help cook and created tension in the group.
I tried several strategies to get them to assist, such as planning out what we would make with them and discussing who would get what cupcake afterward.
In the end, we agreed upon effort equals reward, and for each task each person assisted in, the more cupcakes the person would get.
In retrospect, I realize that the strategies I tried were not entirely effective, and I would try different approaches now.
For example, I would try to understand the underlying reasons for their resistance and address those issues directly.
Additionally, I would focus on building a positive and collaborative team environment and fostering open communication to prevent tensions and resistance in the future.
In conclusion, this blog post has highlighted my personal journey of self-discovery, which has helped me identify my values, strengths, and limitations.
I have also shared a significant ethical decision I made and how my values influenced my decision.
Additionally, I have discussed my strengths and limitations in terms of my learning journey and how they have affected me.
Lastly, I have shared a scenario where I faced resistance and tension while working with others and how I would handle the situation differently now.
