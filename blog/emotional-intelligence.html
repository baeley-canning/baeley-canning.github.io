Emotional-Intelligence

IQ (intelligence quotient) is a measure of cognitive abilities, such as problem-solving and logical reasoning.

EQ (emotional intelligence quotient) is a measure of emotional abilities, such as recognizing and managing emotions in oneself and others.

While IQ is important for certain types of tasks and decision-making, EQ is important for interacting effectively with others, managing relationships, and understanding the emotional consequences of one's actions.

They are both important for overall success, and one's EQ and IQ can complement each other in various settings.
